# UK-Greenhouse-Gas-Emissions-Analysis-
The objective was to identify major emission contributors within the transport sector, evaluate long-term emission trends across economic sectors, compare emissions between selected service industries, and assess methane emission reductions within key industrial sectors.
## Business Questions
The analysis was designed to answer the following questions:

1. Which transport activity contributed the most to UK greenhouse gas emissions between 1990 and 2024?
2. Which economic sectors experienced the largest increases or decreases in emissions over time?
3. Which sector achieved the greatest reduction in methane (CH₄) emissions over the study period?
   
## Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel
- Descriptive Statistics
- Trend Analysis
- Linear Regression (Slope Analysis)
- Independent Samples T-Test
- Data Visualization

## Dataset

Source: Office for National Statistics (ONS)

Dataset: UK Atmospheric Emissions – Greenhouse Gas Emissions by Economic Sector

Period Covered: 1990–2024

Metrics: Greenhouse Gas Emissions (thousand tonnes CO₂ equivalent)

### Long-Term Emission Trends

The UK recorded a substantial reduction in greenhouse gas emissions between 1990 and 2024.

Total UK emissions declined by an average of 11,438 thousand tonnes CO₂e annually.

* Largest Annual Reductions
| Sector | Annual Change (Thousand Tonnes CO₂e) |
|---------|---------:|
| Electricity, Gas and Steam Supply | -3,614 |
| Manufacturing | -3,404 |
| Water Supply and Waste Management | -1,964 |
| Mining and Quarrying | -1,114 |

These reductions reflect improvements in energy generation, efficiency initiatives, and the long-term decline of carbon-intensive industries.

* Largest Annual Increases
| Sector | Annual Change (Thousand Tonnes CO₂e) |
|---------|---------:|
| Construction | +84 |
| Wholesale and Retail Trade | +67 |
| Accommodation and Food Services | +34 |
| Administrative and Support Services | +10 |
| Real Estate Activities | +9 |
The increase within service-oriented sectors reflects structural changes in the UK economy over time.

## Business Insights
1. Aviation remains the most emissions-intensive transport activity.
2. Energy generation and manufacturing achieved the largest emission reductions.
3. Service industries are becoming increasingly important contributors to national emissions.
4. Healthcare operations have substantially higher emissions than education-related activities.
5. Declining methane emissions reflect successful environmental policies and industrial transformation within the UK.
## Recommendations
1. Continue investment in low-carbon energy infrastructure.
2. Accelerate decarbonisation initiatives within the aviation sector.
3. Develop emission reduction strategies for growing service industries.
4. Expand methane monitoring programmes within industrial operations.
5. Prioritize sustainability initiatives within healthcare facilities due to their relatively high emission footprint.
